1.0.Design

With the aim of understanding the time to end of postpartum abstinence and factors predicting the duration, the following analysis plan was obtained.

  • Statistical signiﬁcance was determined at 5%. R Programming Language and Excel were used for data cleaning and SPSS V25 was for all the data analysis.

  • Descriptive statistics were used to summarize findings: means and standard deviations for numerical variables, and frequencies and percentages for categorical variables.

  • The timing of sexual intercourse resumption was categorized into resumption within 1 & 2 weeks, 2 & 3 weeks and 5 & 6 weeks.

  • To assess factors associated with and determining postpartum abstinence, The chis square test, Binary logistic regression, Cox Regression and Kaplan Meir was performed to determine the predictors of early resumption of sexual intercourse after child birth. The predictors of Age, Tribe, Occupation and Parity were reclassified as shown in table 1 below concatenating minor categories into ‘Others’

2.0.The rationale for use of survival analysis

Survival analysis was adopted in the analysis of this study because of the nature of our dependent variable, time to resumption of sex after childbirth, which is a time variable.The Kaplan Meier Product Limit method was used to estimate the survival and hazard function due to its ability to take censored observation into consideration. For those who had resumed sex after childbirth, the survival time is the time period they waited after childbirth before resuming sexual intercourse. However, not all women who were studied had ended postpartum abstinence as at the time of the survey. The duration of postpartum abstinence of these sets of women were right censored as of the survey date and were included in the analysis

The survival function is the probability that a woman will continue postpartum abstinence after a particular time t, was measured in months was estimated and plotted. In this study, the S(t) meaning the probability that. The hazard ratio, h(t) is the instantaneous failure rate at which a woman who had not resumed sexual intercourse by time t, will resume sexual intercourse immediately after time t.

The Log-rank test was used to compare the survival experience between diﬀerent categories of the characteristics studied. We estimated the Incidence rate (IR), which is the probability that a woman will resume sexual activity after a childbirth per unit time (week).

Furthermore, the survival time was modeled with respect to certain prognostic factors using the Cox proportional hazard regression. The coeﬃcients in the Cox regression model indicates the changes expected in the duration of postpartum abstinence in one category of a variable compared with another category. The hazard ratio (HR), expressed as the exponential of the coeﬃcients, indicates a higher likelihood of exposure to event of interest if HR > 1, HR < 1 implies lower exposure; while HR =1 suggests no signiﬁcant diﬀerence in exposure. Variables signiﬁcant in the independent Cox regression were used in the multiple Cox regression while controlling for co founders to generate the adjusted HR.

3.1. Uni-variate for Participants Demographic factors.

Only 145 (53.7%) of the participants were subjected to an intervention and 125(46.3%) were in the control arm. As shown in figure 1 below, Fewer participants (7.79%) resumed intercourse in the intervention arm compared to the control group (27.05%)

Table 2 shows the background characteristics of the participants in the control and intervention arm. 37% and 43.3% were of the Christian religious denomination in the control and intervention group respectively; 39.6% and 43.7% were of the House Wives for the control and intervention group respectively; and 36.0%, 31.7% had delivered their second child for the control and intervention groups respectively.

There were no observed differences between the two arms with to all characteristics (p >0.05) as shown by the chi squared values and t values, however, there is a significant difference in the mean time to resumption of intercourse between control and intervention group. (t = -5.036, p = 0.000).

5.0. Comparing the survival experience between diﬀerent categories of the characteristics using the Chi Squared Log Rank Test (adjusted for group)

In Table 5 above, we present the outcome of log-rank test used to determine the signiﬁcance of diﬀerences in the duration of resumption of sex after birth among the diﬀerent categories of the explanatory variables.

From the Log-rank test as shown in Table 2 above, shows that there exists a signiﬁcant diﬀerence in survival distribution of time to resumption of sex after birth by Occupation [log rank = 9.094, p = 0.003]. The Log-rank test shows that all explanatory variables considered in this study never had a signiﬁcantly diﬀerent survival distribution except the variable of coerced to have sex [log rank = 5.158, p = 0.023].

6.0. Hazard Ratio

A significant adjusted hazard ratio was observed for working women (aHR = 0.365, p=0.007) compared with those that are housewives, hence working women were 63.5% less likely to resume sex compared to house wives. Those coerced to have sex (aHR = 2.789, p=0.036), thus women coerced to have sex are having a higher likely hood to resume sex after birth.

Additionally, the adjusted hazard ratio of earlier resumption of sexual activity after birth for the intervention arm (aHR = 0.286, 95% CI: [0.147,0.555]); hence participants subject to intervention were 85.3% less likely to resume sex after childbirth compared to their counterparts in the control arm.

7.0. Multivariate Analysis

The Overall mean survival time is 4.6 weeks and the overall median time between date of delivery and resumption of sex after birth is 5 weeks.

The estimated mean time until resumption of Intercourse is 4 weeks for the control group and 5.7 weeks for the intervention group hence participants in the intervention group were more likely to delay ERSP compared to those in the control arm

The median time between date of delivery and resumption of sex after birth is 4 weeks for the control group compared 6 weeks for the intervention group.

From the plot above, the survival probability is lower for the control group at all time points so, the participants in the control group are less likely to survive.

Using the Log Rank (Mantel-Cox) test (20.606, P =0.000), As the p-value is less than 0.05, we conclude that there is significant evidence of a difference in survival times for participant in the control group and those in the intervention group. The estimated time until resumption of intercourse is 4 weeks for the control group and 5.7 weeks for intervention group, i.e., participant in the intervention group have an increased chance of survival.
